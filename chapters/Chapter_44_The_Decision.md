**Chapter Forty-Four — The Decision**

The next morning, they met Elias again.

This time, Note didn’t hesitate.

"We’ve chosen," he said.

Elias raised an eyebrow.

"Which one?"

Note pointed to the farthest location on the map.

"This one."

Elias was quiet for a moment. Then he nodded.

"It’s the hardest path," he said. "But it’s also the one with the best chance of letting you disappear for a while… if that’s still what you want."

Soraya shook her head.

"We don’t want to disappear," she said. "We want to stop running. This place gives us the most time to prepare before we have to face whatever’s coming."

Elias looked between them.

"You’re sure about this?"

Note met his eyes.

"We’re sure."

Elias studied them for a long moment, then gave a slow nod.

"Alright," he said. "Then we stop talking about leaving. We start getting ready to leave."

He rolled up the map.

"You have two weeks," he said. "Maybe three if we’re lucky. After that, staying here becomes more dangerous than leaving."

Note and Soraya looked at each other.

Then Note turned back to Elias.

"We’ll be ready," he said.

Elias gave them both a long, serious look.

"I hope so," he said. "Because once you leave this sanctuary… the world is going to know the First Flame is awake. And it’s not going to forget."

He turned and walked away, leaving Note and Soraya standing alone with the weight of the decision they had made.

Soraya reached over and took Note’s hand.

"No more waiting," she said.

Note squeezed her hand.

"No more waiting."
