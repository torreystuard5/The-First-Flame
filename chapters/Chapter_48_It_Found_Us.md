**Chapter Forty-Eight — It Found Us**

They didn’t make it another full day.

Just after midday, the temperature dropped sharply. The wind died. The forest around them went unnaturally still.

Note stopped walking.

Soraya felt it at the same time. She slowly reached for the knife at her hip, her other hand finding Note’s arm.

"It’s here," she whispered.

Note’s eyes were already glowing — a steady, dangerous blue-green. The fire inside him wasn’t restless anymore.

It was awake.

And it was angry.

A low, rattling sound echoed through the trees ahead of them. Not footsteps. Something dragging. Something heavy.

Then it stepped out of the shadows between two pines.

It wasn’t human.

It looked like it had once been a person, but that was a long time ago. Its skin was gray and stretched too tight over its bones. Its eyes were empty black pits, and its mouth was a jagged, torn line. Black smoke curled from its shoulders like torn wings.

It tilted its head when it saw them.

Then it smiled.

Note moved in front of Soraya without thinking.

The creature’s voice came out wrong — like multiple voices speaking at once, all of them broken.

"You smell like him," it rasped. "The First Flame. We’ve been looking for you for a very long time."

Soraya’s grip on Note’s arm tightened.

"What is it?" she asked quietly.

Note didn’t take his eyes off the creature.

"Something that used to be human," he said. "Now it’s just… hungry."

The thing took another step forward. The ground beneath its feet turned black and dead.

"You can’t run anymore," it said, almost gently. "We’ve already tasted your fire. We know where you’re going. We’ll be waiting there too."

Note’s flames began to rise along his arms.

"Then you should’ve stayed hidden," he said, voice low.

The creature’s empty eyes flicked to Soraya.

"And you brought your little anchor with you. How sweet." It grinned wider. "We’ll enjoy breaking her first."

Soraya’s voice was cold when she answered.

"You won’t touch him."

The creature laughed — a wet, horrible sound.

Then it lunged."
