**Chapter Forty-Two — The Map**

The next morning, Note, Soraya, and Elias sat at a long wooden table in one of the quieter rooms of the sanctuary. A large, old map was spread across the surface, weighted down at the corners with stones.

Elias tapped a finger on a spot deep in the mountains.

"This is where we are," he said. "Remote enough that most things don’t come looking. But it won’t stay that way forever."

Note leaned over the map.

"Where do we go from here?"

Elias didn’t answer right away. He looked at both of them.

"That depends on what you’re willing to do," he said. "You can keep running. Find another quiet place, try to stay hidden. Or…"

He looked directly at Note.

"You can stop running."

Soraya spoke before Note could.

"What does that actually look like?"

Elias was honest.

"It looks like drawing attention. It looks like letting the flame be seen. And it looks like accepting that some things will come for you the moment they know you exist."

Note was quiet for a long moment.

"I’m tired of hiding," he said finally. "But I’m not stupid. We can’t just walk out there and hope for the best."

Soraya placed her hand over his on the table.

"We need somewhere to go," she said. "A real destination. Not just away from here."

Elias nodded.

"There are old places," he said. "Sanctuaries that were abandoned long before this one. Some of them still have power in the land. If you’re serious about stopping, one of those might be worth finding."

Note looked at Soraya.

She met his eyes and gave a small nod.

"We’re serious," Note said.

Elias studied them both for a moment, then sighed.

"Alright. Then we start making a real plan. Not training. Not memories. Actual movement."

He rolled the map slightly and pointed to a few marked locations.

"These are the places worth considering. But none of them are close. And none of them are safe."

Soraya looked at Note again.

He squeezed her hand once under the table.

"Then we pick one," he said. "And we get ready to go."

Elias gave a slow nod.

"Tomorrow," he said. "We start choosing."
