**Chapter Forty-Nine — We Fight Together**

Note moved first.

He stepped forward and slammed both palms into the ground. A wave of blue-green fire exploded outward, forcing the creature back. It shrieked as the flames licked across its gray skin, but it didn’t fall.

Soraya moved at the same time.

She didn’t try to match the fire. Instead, she did what she did best — she anchored. She placed one hand on Note’s back and pushed her own power outward, steady and golden. The moment she touched him, the fire stabilized. It stopped flaring wildly and became focused.

The creature circled them, faster than something that looked that broken should be able to move.

It came at Soraya first.

Note reacted instantly. He threw his hand out and a spear of fire shot forward, slamming into the creature’s side and knocking it off course. At the same time, Soraya raised her other hand and sent a sharp pulse of golden light into its chest.

The thing staggered.

It looked at them with something almost like surprise.

"You’ve learned to share it," it hissed. "That’s new."

Note’s voice was steady.

"We’re done running from things like you."

The creature attacked again — faster this time. It moved like smoke, appearing behind Soraya. She spun, but she wasn’t fast enough.

Note was.

He appeared between them in a burst of flame, grabbing the creature by the throat. The fire in his hand burned white-hot. The thing screamed as its skin began to crack and burn.

But it wasn’t dying easily.

It drove a clawed hand into Note’s side. Pain flared white-hot across his ribs.

Soraya reacted without thinking.

She slammed both hands against the creature’s back and poured everything she had into it — not fire, but pure, steady light. The creature howled as her power burned through it from behind while Note’s fire consumed it from the front.

For several long seconds, the forest was filled with screaming.

Then the creature finally collapsed into a pile of ash and black smoke.

Note dropped to one knee, breathing hard. Blood was soaking through his shirt where the claws had caught him.

Soraya was at his side instantly, hands shaking as she pressed them against the wound.

"Note—"

"I’m okay," he said through gritted teeth. "It’s not deep."

She didn’t look convinced, but she didn’t argue. Instead, she helped him sit back against a tree and quickly tore a strip from her outer layer to wrap around his side.

They stayed like that for a while, the only sound their breathing and the wind moving through the trees.

Finally, Soraya spoke.

"That was just one."

Note nodded slowly, his face pale but determined.

"Yeah."

He looked at her.

"They’re going to keep coming."

Soraya met his eyes without flinching.

"Then we keep fighting," she said. "Together."

Note reached up and touched her face gently.

"Together."
