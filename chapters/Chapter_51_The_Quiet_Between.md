**Chapter Fifty-One — The Quiet Between**

They traveled slowly the next day.

Note’s side was bandaged tightly, and while the wound wasn’t deep, it still hurt when he moved too much. Soraya stayed close to him the entire time, one hand often resting lightly on his arm as if she was afraid he might disappear if she let go.

They didn’t talk much during the day. The forest felt too watchful. But that night, when they made camp in a small clearing, Soraya finally spoke.

"I keep thinking about what that thing said," she admitted quietly. "That it had been looking for you for a long time."

Note was sitting against a tree, staring into the small fire he’d made. The flames were low and controlled.

"I know," he said. "It didn’t feel like it was lying."

Soraya pulled her knees up to her chest and wrapped her arms around them.

"Does that scare you?"

Note was quiet for a moment.

"Yeah," he admitted. "Not because it’s hunting me. But because it knew what I am. It called me the First Flame like it had heard the name before."

Soraya looked at him.

"Do you think there are more of them?"

Note nodded slowly.

"I think that was just a scout. Or a hunter sent ahead. The way it talked… it sounded like it was part of something bigger."

Soraya was quiet for a long time after that. Then she moved closer and rested her head against his shoulder.

"I’m not going to let them take you," she said softly. "Whatever they are. Whatever they want. I’m not going to let them have you."

Note wrapped an arm around her.

"I know," he said. "And I’m not going to let them touch you either."

They sat like that for a long time, the small fire crackling between them and the dark forest pressing in from all sides.

For the first time since they left the sanctuary, the silence between them didn’t feel heavy with uncertainty.

It felt like a promise."
