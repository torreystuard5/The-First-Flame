**Chapter Sixty — The First Real Choice**

That night, they sat in the common room again.

The fire burned low in the hearth while they talked about what they had found.

"We can’t just keep exploring and hoping we figure things out by accident," Note said. "We need a plan."

Soraya nodded.

"I agree. But I also don’t think we should rush into anything. This place is old. It might not like being pushed."

Note leaned back in his chair.

"So what do we do?"

Soraya thought for a moment.

"We start small. We keep training, but we also start trying to understand what those tablets are saying. And we stop treating every day like it might be the last peaceful one we get."

She looked at him.

"We came here to stop running. That means we actually have to live here like we mean it."

Note was quiet for a while. Then he nodded.

"Okay," he said. "We start building something real."

Soraya gave him a small smile.

"Together?"

Note looked at her and shook his head lightly.

"We don’t have to say it every time anymore."

Soraya’s smile grew a little.

"Good. Because I meant it the first hundred times."

Note let out a quiet laugh.

They sat in comfortable silence after that, the fire crackling softly between them. The sanctuary was still and old around them, but it no longer felt like a temporary stop.

It was starting to feel like a place they could actually stay.

And for the first time, that didn’t feel like a dangerous thing to hope for."
