**Chapter Fifty-Four — The Quiet Strength**

They traveled steadily over the next two days.

The mountains grew steeper and colder, but Note and Soraya kept a consistent pace. They didn’t push themselves to exhaustion, but they also didn’t linger in one place for too long. Every night they made camp, ate, and rested. Every morning they kept moving.

Something had shifted between them after the fight in the clearing. It wasn’t loud or dramatic. It was quieter — the way they moved together, the way they communicated without needing to explain everything. When Note’s flames started to feel unstable, Soraya would simply place a hand on his arm and the fire would settle. When something felt off in the trees, Note would glance at her and she would already be watching the same spot.

One evening, as they sat beside a small fire, Soraya spoke.

"I used to think strength meant never being afraid," she said quietly. "Now I think it means knowing you’re afraid and still choosing to keep going."

Note looked at her across the flames.

"You’ve always been strong," he said. "Even when you were scared. Especially then."

Soraya gave him a small smile.

"You’ve changed too. You used to carry everything like it was only yours to hold. Now you actually let me help."

Note nodded slowly.

"I think I finally understand that I don’t have to do this alone. The fire… it’s stronger when I’m not trying to control it by myself. When I trust you with it."

Soraya reached over and took his hand.

"We’re not the same people who left the first sanctuary," she said. "And I don’t think we’re supposed to be."

Note squeezed her hand gently.

"No," he agreed. "We’re not."

They sat in comfortable silence after that, watching the fire burn low. For the first time in a long while, the quiet didn’t feel like they were waiting for something to go wrong.

It felt like they were preparing for what came next — together."
