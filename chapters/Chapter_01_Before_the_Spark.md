# Chapter One — Before the Spark

Note fell.

The world tilted sideways in a sudden, sickening rush. One moment he was walking down the school hallway, the next his foot caught on nothing and the ground rushed up to meet him. The impact knocked the air from his lungs. For a few seconds, all he could do was lie there on the cold tile, dazed, while the chatter of students swirled around him like distant noise.

When he finally lifted his head, most people were just standing there, staring. A few whispered. No one moved to help.

Except one.

A flash of black fabric cut through the crowd. Soraya dropped to her knees beside him, her niqab fluttering as she reached out with shaking hands. Her dark eyes were wide with something that looked far too much like fear for someone who had just met him.

"Note! Oh God, Note..." Her voice was low, urgent, stripped of its usual calm. "Are you okay? Please... tell me you're alright."

He blinked up at her, still trying to catch his breath. "Your eyes..."

She froze for half a second, then looked away quickly, but not before he saw the flicker of something old and heavy in her gaze.

"I'm just... I was scared," she whispered. "I thought... I thought something happened to you."

Note studied her face. Even through the niqab, he could see the tension in her shoulders, the way her gloved hands hovered like she wanted to touch him but was afraid to.

"I'm okay," he said quietly. "Thanks to you."

She let out a shaky breath and helped him sit up. For a moment, neither of them moved. The rest of the hallway had already started to lose interest, but Soraya stayed right there, close enough that he could smell the faint floral scent she wore.

"You shouldn't fall like that," she said, almost to herself. "Not you."

Note didn't ask what she meant. He just looked at her and felt something settle into place — something that had been waiting a very long time.
