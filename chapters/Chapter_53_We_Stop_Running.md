**Chapter Fifty-Three — We Stop Running**

They didn’t wait for nightfall.

As soon as the decision was made, Note and Soraya turned to face the trees. The dark shapes that had been following them for days slowly began to emerge — twisted, gray-skinned things that looked like they had once been human but had long since been broken and remade by something cruel.

There were at least twenty of them.

Note stepped forward, flames already burning steadily along both arms. Soraya stood just behind his right shoulder, her golden light glowing brighter than it had before.

One of the larger creatures broke through and came straight at Note. He met it head-on, grabbing it by the throat as white-hot fire surged through his arm. The thing screamed as it burned from the inside out.

Soraya handled two more on her own, using quick, precise bursts of light to keep them off balance until Note could finish them.

When the last creature finally fell, the clearing went quiet.

Note stood in the center of it, breathing hard, flames still flickering faintly along his arms. Soraya walked over to him, wiping blood from a cut on her cheek. She looked at the bodies scattered around them, then back at Note.

"They’re getting bolder," she said.

Note nodded.

"They’re also getting desperate. They know we’re not running anymore."

Soraya looked out toward the direction they had been heading.

"We’re close to the new sanctuary," she said. "Maybe another week if we push."

Note followed her gaze.

"Then we push," he said. "No more letting them decide when we fight. We keep moving. If they come for us again, we hit them harder."

Soraya looked at him for a moment, then gave a small, tired smile.

"I like this version of us better."

Note let the last of the flames die down and reached over to take her hand.

"Me too."

They didn’t stay in the clearing long. They cleaned up what they could, then kept moving — deeper into the mountains, toward the place they had chosen.

Whatever was hunting them was still out there.

But for the first time, Note and Soraya weren’t just reacting to it.

They were moving forward on their own terms."
