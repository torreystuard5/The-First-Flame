**Chapter Fifty-Five — Arrival**

They pushed hard over the next two days.

The terrain grew harsher and the air thinner, but Note and Soraya didn’t slow down. They moved with purpose now — not out of fear, but because they had chosen this path. Every step brought them closer to the place they had decided to make their stand.

On the afternoon of the twelfth day since leaving the original sanctuary, they crested a high ridge and finally saw it.

The new sanctuary sat nestled in a wide valley between two towering peaks. It was older and more worn than the place they had left behind — stone buildings half-covered in moss and snow, with ancient symbols carved into the walls that still faintly glowed when the light hit them a certain way. A single, wide stone bridge connected the main cluster of buildings to the rest of the valley, and a thin river ran alongside it.

It wasn’t beautiful in the traditional sense, but it felt… solid. Hidden. Protected.

Note stopped at the edge of the ridge and stared down at it.

"That’s it," he said quietly.

Soraya stood beside him, her eyes scanning the valley below.

"It looks abandoned," she said. "But not dead."

Note nodded.

"Elias said it still has power in the land. We just have to hope it’s enough."

They stood there for a moment longer, taking it in. Then Soraya turned to him.

"We made it."

Note looked at her and gave a small, tired smile.

"Yeah. We did."

They descended the ridge together. As they crossed the old stone bridge and stepped into the sanctuary grounds, the air felt different — heavier, but not in a threatening way. It felt like the land itself was aware of them.

Note reached out and took Soraya’s hand.

"Whatever happens next," he said, "we face it together."

Soraya squeezed his hand once.

"Together."

They walked deeper into the sanctuary side by side, leaving the open mountains behind them.

For the first time in weeks, they had somewhere to stand.

And they were no longer running."
