**Chapter Forty-Six — Shadows on the Trail**

On the third day, they noticed the first sign.

It was subtle at first — a feeling more than anything else. The kind of cold that settled in the bones even when the sun was out. Note kept glancing over his shoulder, and Soraya’s hand kept drifting toward the small knife she carried at her hip.

By late afternoon, they both knew they weren’t alone.

They stopped in a small clearing surrounded by thick pine trees. Note stood still, eyes half-closed, feeling the fire inside him. It was restless again — not wild like before, but alert. Watching.

Soraya moved to stand beside him.

"You feel it too?"

Note nodded slowly.

"Something’s been following us since this morning. It’s staying just far enough back that I can’t see it clearly."

Soraya’s voice was calm, but her eyes were sharp.

"Do we keep moving or make a stand?"

Note was quiet for a moment, then shook his head.

"We keep moving. For now. If we stop and fight, we might be playing into whatever’s tracking us. We need to reach better ground first."

Soraya nodded once.

"Then we move faster."

They pushed on through the rest of the day and into the early evening, only stopping when the light became too dangerous to travel by. That night, they didn’t light a fire. They sat close together in the dark, speaking in low voices.

Note kept one hand resting on the ground, feeling the faint pulse of the fire beneath his skin.

"It’s getting closer," he said quietly.

Soraya didn’t ask how he knew. She simply tightened her grip on his arm.

"Then we need to be ready," she whispered.

Note looked out into the darkness between the trees.

"We will be."

But even as he said it, he could feel the flame inside him growing more alert.

Whatever was following them wasn’t just curious.

It was hunting.

And it was getting closer."
