**Chapter Fifty-Nine — What the Walls Remember**

On their fourth day in the sanctuary, they found something.

In one of the deeper rooms, behind a partially collapsed wall, Soraya discovered a set of old stone tablets. They weren’t written in any language she recognized, but the symbols on them matched some of the carvings on the outer walls.

Note stood beside her as she carefully brushed dust off one of the tablets.

"Can you read any of it?" he asked.

Soraya shook her head.

"Not really. But I can feel something from them. It’s not the same as the fire inside you… but it’s connected somehow."

Note reached out and touched one of the tablets. The moment his fingers made contact, the symbols on it glowed faintly blue-green — the same color as his flames.

They both stared at it.

Soraya looked at him.

"It reacted to you."

Note pulled his hand back slowly.

"This place knows what I am."

Soraya studied the glowing symbols for a long moment.

"Maybe it doesn’t just know what you are," she said quietly. "Maybe it’s been waiting for someone like you."

Note didn’t answer. He just stared at the tablet as the glow slowly faded.

Whatever this sanctuary was, it clearly wasn’t empty.

It had been waiting for something.

Or someone."
