**Chapter Forty-Five — The Road Out**

They left at dawn.

There was no grand goodbye. No long speeches. Elias simply met them at the edge of the sanctuary grounds with two packs already prepared and a quiet nod.

"Two weeks," he reminded them. "Maybe less. Don’t waste time."

Note adjusted the strap on his shoulder and looked back at the buildings one last time. Soraya stood beside him, her niqab catching the early light, her gloved hand finding his without a word.

They didn’t look back again.

The first day was quiet.

They moved fast but carefully, following old mountain paths that Elias had marked for them. The higher they climbed, the thinner the air became. By midday, the sanctuary was already hidden behind the ridges.

That night, they made camp in a narrow pass between two cliffs. The fire Note made was small and controlled — just enough to keep them warm. Soraya sat close to him, her shoulder pressed against his as they ate in silence.

After a while, she spoke.

"It feels strange," she said quietly. "Leaving."

Note stared into the flames.

"It does."

She hesitated, then asked the question they’d both been avoiding.

"Do you think something’s already following us?"

Note didn’t answer right away. He stared at the fire for a long moment before speaking.

"I don’t know," he said. "But the flame feels… sharper tonight. Like it’s watching something I can’t see yet."

Soraya moved closer and rested her head against his shoulder.

"If something’s coming," she said, "we’ll deal with it."

Note wrapped an arm around her.

"Together."

They sat like that for a long time, the small fire crackling between them and the vast darkness of the mountains pressing in from all sides.

Neither of them said it out loud, but they both felt it.

They were no longer just running.

They were being hunted.

And the road ahead was only going to get darker."
