**Chapter Fifty — After the Fire**

They didn’t move for the rest of the day.

Note’s wound wasn’t life-threatening, but it was deep enough that walking would’ve been dangerous. They found a small overhang of rock and made a hidden camp. Soraya cleaned and wrapped the injury as best she could while Note rested against the stone wall, watching her work.

When she finished, she sat beside him and leaned her head against his uninjured side.

Neither of them spoke for a long time.

Eventually, Note broke the silence.

"I almost lost control back there."

Soraya didn’t lift her head.

"I know."

"I wanted to burn everything," he admitted quietly. "Not just that thing. Everything. The trees. The sky. I wanted it all gone."

Soraya was quiet for a moment.

"But you didn’t."

"Because of you," he said. "You kept me here."

She lifted her head and looked at him.

"I’m not going to let the fire take you," she said. "Not now. Not ever. Even if I have to stand in front of it every single time."

Note studied her face.

"I don’t want you to have to do that."

"I know," she said softly. "But I will. Because I love you. And because we’re in this together now. Not just you carrying it. Us."

Note reached over and took her hand.

"I don’t know what’s waiting at the end of this road," he said. "But I know I don’t want to face it without you."

Soraya squeezed his hand.

"You won’t have to."
