**Chapter Fifty-Eight — What We’re Building**

The next morning, they returned to the main courtyard.

Note practiced moving the fire in controlled patterns while Soraya watched and occasionally offered feedback. She wasn’t trying to guide the flames the way she had during their fights on the road. Instead, she was learning how her presence affected the space around them.

After a while, she spoke.

"I don’t think we should train the same way we did before."

Note lowered his hands and looked at her.

"What do you mean?"

Soraya walked over to him.

"At the last place, we were always reacting. We trained because we were scared of what was coming. Here… I think we should train because we’re choosing to get stronger. Not because we’re afraid of losing control."

Note was quiet for a moment, then nodded slowly.

"I like that."

He looked around at the old stone walls.

"This place doesn’t feel like it was built for people who are running. It feels like it was built for people who decided to stay."

Soraya smiled faintly.

"Then let’s stop training like we’re still running."

They went back to practicing, but the energy between them felt different. Less desperate. More deliberate. They weren’t just trying to survive anymore.

They were starting to build something."
