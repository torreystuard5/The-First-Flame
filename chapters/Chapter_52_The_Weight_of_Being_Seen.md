**Chapter Fifty-Two — The Weight of Being Seen**

They didn’t speak much during the day.

Note kept his pace steady, but Soraya could tell he was thinking. His eyes kept drifting to the trees, and the fire inside him felt restless in a way it hadn’t since they started traveling together. By late afternoon, she finally stopped walking.

"Tell me what you’re not saying," she said.

Note looked at her, then sighed and rubbed the back of his neck.

"They’re not just following us anymore," he said quietly. "They’re herding us."

Soraya frowned.

"What do you mean?"

Note gestured at the path ahead.

"Every time we try to change direction, something shifts in the trees. They’re not attacking. They’re guiding us. Like they want us to go somewhere specific."

Soraya was quiet for a moment, processing that.

"So if we keep going like this… we’re walking into whatever they want us to walk into."

Note nodded.

"Yeah."

He looked at her.

"We have two real choices. We can keep following the path they’re clearing for us and hope we’re strong enough for whatever’s waiting at the end… or we can break the pattern. Force them to react instead of us."

Soraya studied his face.

"What would breaking the pattern look like?"

Note was honest.

"It would mean stopping. Not to fight — not yet. But to make them come to us on *our* terms. We pick the ground. We choose the moment. We stop letting them decide how this goes."

Soraya looked out at the trees. The shadows between them felt heavier than they had that morning.

She turned back to Note.

"If we do that," she said, "there’s no going back to just traveling. It becomes a real fight."

Note met her eyes without hesitation.

"I’m tired of running."

Soraya looked at him for a moment, then nodded.

"Okay," she said. "Then we stop letting them push us."

Note gave her a small, tired smile.

"You sure?"

"I’m sure," she said. "I’m tired of feeling like prey. If they want us, they can come get us. But it’ll be on our terms."

Note reached over and took her hand.

"Together," he said.

Soraya squeezed his hand once.

"Together."

They didn’t keep walking.

Instead, they turned off the path and moved deeper into the forest, deliberately choosing difficult terrain. They didn’t try to hide their trail. They wanted to be found — but only when *they* were ready.

Whatever was hunting them had been playing a game.

Now Note and Soraya were done playing by someone else’s rules."
