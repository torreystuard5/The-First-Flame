**Chapter Fifty-Six — The Old Stones**

The sanctuary was quiet when they stepped inside.

Unlike the last place, there were no people waiting to greet them. No Elias. No familiar faces. Just old stone buildings, faded symbols carved into the walls, and the low sound of wind moving through empty corridors. It felt less like a sanctuary and more like a place that had been left behind a long time ago.

Note and Soraya walked slowly through the main courtyard. The stones beneath their feet were worn smooth from years of use, but patches of moss and snow still clung to the corners. Some of the symbols on the walls gave off a faint glow when they passed, reacting to their presence.

Soraya ran her gloved hand along one of the carvings.

"It’s older than the last one," she said. "I can feel it. The power here is… different. Quieter."

Note nodded as he looked around.

"It feels like it’s been waiting."

They found what looked like living quarters in one of the central buildings. The rooms were simple but intact — stone walls, old wooden furniture, and thick blankets still folded on the beds. It wasn’t luxurious, but it was shelter.

They chose two rooms next to each other. Neither of them said anything about it, but they both seemed to understand they didn’t want to be too far apart.

Later, they stood on a wide balcony overlooking the valley. The sun was starting to set behind the mountains, casting long shadows across the stone.

Soraya leaned against the railing.

"So… what now?"

Note was quiet for a moment before answering.

"We rest. We figure out what this place can actually do for us. And we stop reacting to everything that comes at us."

He looked out at the valley.

"I’m tired of always being on the defensive. If something comes for us here, we deal with it. But I don’t want to spend every day waiting for the next fight."

Soraya nodded slowly.

"I agree. We came here to stop running. That means we actually have to start living like it."

She glanced at him.

"We should probably start by learning what this place is capable of. The symbols on the walls… they reacted to us. That has to mean something."

Note gave a small nod.

"Tomorrow," he said. "We start figuring this place out."

Soraya looked back out at the mountains.

"It feels strange," she admitted. "Being somewhere new. No Elias. No training grounds we already know. Just… us and whatever this place is."

Note was quiet for a few seconds.

"Yeah," he said. "It does feel strange. But it also feels like we finally have some room to breathe."

They stood on the balcony a little longer as the sun dipped lower. The sanctuary was still and old around them, but it didn’t feel empty in a bad way. It felt like it was waiting to see what they would do with it.

For the first time in a long time, the road ahead didn’t feel like it was being decided for them.

It felt like they were finally the ones choosing."
