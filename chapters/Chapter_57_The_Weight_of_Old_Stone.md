**Chapter Fifty-Seven — The Weight of Old Stone**

The first full day in the new sanctuary was quiet.

Note and Soraya spent most of it walking through the buildings, getting a sense of the layout. The place was larger than it first appeared. There were training grounds that had clearly been used for something more intense than what they had done at the last sanctuary — deep grooves in the stone where powerful energy had once been channeled, and faded symbols that still reacted when Note stepped too close.

Soraya spent time studying the carvings on the walls while Note tested how the fire responded in different areas. It behaved differently here. Calmer in some rooms, sharper in others. It wasn’t fighting him, but it also wasn’t as responsive as it had been during their last fight.

That evening, they sat in one of the larger common rooms. A fire crackled in the old stone hearth, casting long shadows across the walls.

"This place feels heavier than the last one," Note said after a while. "Not in a bad way. Just… older. Like it remembers more."

Soraya nodded, her eyes on the flames.

"I think that’s why Elias sent us here. The last sanctuary was for hiding and training. This one feels like it was built for something else."

Note looked at her.

"Like what?"

She was quiet for a moment before answering.

"Like standing your ground."

Note didn’t reply right away. He just stared into the fire, thinking.

Eventually, he said, "Then we should probably start figuring out what this place actually wants from us."

Soraya gave a small nod.

"Tomorrow."
