**Chapter Forty-Three — The Weight of Choosing**

That night, Note and Soraya sat together in their room. The map Elias had shown them earlier was now spread across their small table, lit by a single lamp.

They had been staring at it for almost an hour.

Soraya finally spoke.

"None of these places feel safe."

Note let out a quiet breath.

"I know."

She traced one of the marked locations with her finger.

"This one is the farthest. It would take weeks to reach, even if we move fast. But it’s also the most hidden."

Note looked at the other two options.

"These are closer," he said. "But they’re also more exposed. If something is already looking for us, we might lead it straight there."

Soraya leaned back in her chair and rubbed her eyes.

"I hate this," she admitted. "Every choice feels like we’re picking which danger we want to walk into."

Note reached over and took her hand.

"I know," he said quietly. "But we can’t stay here. And we can’t keep running forever either."

Soraya looked at him.

"What if we pick wrong?"

Note was honest.

"Then we deal with it. Together."

She studied his face for a long moment, then nodded slowly.

"Okay," she said. "Then we pick the one that gives us the best chance to actually stop running. Even if it’s harder to get to."

Note looked back down at the map.

"The farthest one," he said.

Soraya nodded.

"The farthest one."

They sat in silence for a while after that. Eventually, Soraya spoke again, her voice softer.

"I’m scared, Note."

He squeezed her hand.

"Me too."

She leaned her head against his shoulder.

"But I’d rather be scared and moving than scared and waiting."

Note rested his cheek against the top of her head.

"Then we move."
