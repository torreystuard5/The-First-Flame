**Chapter Forty-Seven — The First Sign**

They found the first real evidence just after sunrise on the fifth day.

A tree at the edge of their camp had been marked. Not with a blade or fire — but with something colder. The bark was gray and brittle, as if the life had been drained from it. When Note touched it, the fire in his veins reacted sharply, pulling back like it had been burned.

Soraya stood beside him, staring at the mark.

"What did this?"

Note didn’t answer right away. He stared at the dead tree, his jaw tight.

"Something that doesn’t like the fire," he said finally. "Something that’s trying to follow the trail we’re leaving behind."

Soraya looked around at the surrounding forest. Everything seemed normal… but it felt wrong. Too quiet.

"We can’t keep traveling like this," she said. "If it’s tracking us this easily, we’re leading it straight to the new sanctuary."

Note nodded.

"I know."

He turned to face her.

"We have two choices. We can keep running and hope we lose it… or we can stop and make it regret following us."

Soraya met his eyes without hesitation.

"I’m tired of running."

Note gave a small, grim smile.

"Me too."

He looked back at the marked tree one last time.

"Then we stop running."

The flame inside him burned hotter at those words — not wild, but focused.

Ready.

Whatever was coming for them had finally caught up.

And Note and Soraya were done pretending they were still just trying to survive."
