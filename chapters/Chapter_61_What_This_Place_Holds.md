**Chapter Sixty-One — What This Place Holds**

On their fifth day in the new sanctuary, Note and Soraya returned to the room with the stone tablets.

They had spent the morning clearing more of the collapsed wall and carefully moving the tablets into better light. There were seven in total, each one covered in the same strange symbols that appeared on the outer walls of the sanctuary.

Note stood in front of them while Soraya examined one of the tablets more closely.

"These aren’t just decorations," she said after a while. "They feel like they’re meant to be read. Or used."

Note reached out and touched one of the symbols. It glowed faintly under his fingers again, the same blue-green color as his flames.

"Do you think this place was built for someone like me?" he asked.

Soraya looked at him.

"Maybe. Or maybe it was built for people who carry something like what you carry." She paused. "Either way, I don’t think we ended up here by accident."

Note was quiet for a moment.

"I keep expecting something to go wrong," he admitted. "Like we’re going to wake up tomorrow and something will be attacking us again. But it’s been quiet since we got here."

Soraya nodded.

"I feel it too. But I don’t think that means we should relax completely. I think it means we finally have some space to actually prepare instead of just surviving."

She looked back at the tablets.

"We should start trying to understand these. Even if we can’t read them yet, maybe we can figure out what they do."

Note studied the glowing symbols on the tablet under his hand.

"Alright," he said. "Then that’s what we do. We stop waiting for the next problem to find us. We start figuring out what this place actually is."

Soraya gave a small nod.

They spent the rest of the day in that room, carefully examining the tablets and the symbols on the walls. They didn’t make any big discoveries, but the work felt different from the training they had done before.

It didn’t feel like they were preparing for the next fight.

It felt like they were starting to understand where they were.

And for the first time since they arrived, the sanctuary didn’t feel like just another temporary stop.

It was starting to feel like the place where things would actually begin."
